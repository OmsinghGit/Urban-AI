# Project AERIS

# Competitor Analysis

## Objective

The objective of this analysis is to understand the strengths, limitations, and innovation gaps of existing air quality platforms and identify opportunities for building an AI-first Decision Intelligence Platform for governments and smart cities.

---

# Executive Summary

Existing air quality platforms are highly effective at monitoring and visualizing environmental data. Some platforms provide forecasting and health advisories, while others act as trusted government reporting systems.

However, almost all existing solutions stop at information delivery.

None of the analyzed platforms provide a complete AI-powered decision intelligence workflow capable of identifying pollution causes, recommending interventions, simulating policy outcomes, tracking action effectiveness, and assisting government authorities in operational decision-making. This recurring gap represents the primary opportunity for AERIS. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

# Individual Competitor Analysis

---

## Google Air Quality

### Purpose

Developer-friendly global air quality platform integrated with Google Maps.

### Strengths

- Excellent map visualization
- High spatial resolution
- Historical AQI
- Forecasts up to multiple days
- Strong API ecosystem
- Reliable infrastructure

### Weaknesses

- Designed as a data platform rather than a government operations platform.
- No AI-based pollution source attribution.
- No intervention recommendations.
- No policy simulation.
- No operational workflows for government agencies.

### Innovation Opportunity

Transform monitoring into actionable decision intelligence.

---

## IQAir

### Purpose

Public-facing air quality monitoring and health awareness platform.

### Strengths

- Global AQI coverage
- User-friendly interface
- Strong citizen awareness
- Mobile accessibility

### Weaknesses

- Consumer-focused.
- Limited support for administrative workflows.
- No AI-driven intervention planning.
- No policy optimization.
- No decision automation.

### Innovation Opportunity

Shift focus from public awareness to government action planning.

## Common Limitations

Every analyzed platform shares one or more of the following limitations:

- No AI-powered decision support
- No pollution source attribution
- No intervention ranking
- No policy simulation
- No explainable AI
- No administrative workflows
- No impact measurement
- No action prioritization
- No closed-loop monitoring

These recurring gaps indicate that current systems primarily answer **"What is happening?"** rather than **"Why is it happening?"** or **"What should be done next?"** :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

# AERIS Positioning

AERIS is **not another AQI dashboard**.

It is an AI-powered Urban Environmental Decision Intelligence Platform built specifically for government agencies, municipal corporations, pollution control boards, and smart city authorities.

Instead of simply displaying environmental information, AERIS helps decision makers understand pollution, evaluate possible interventions, simulate policy outcomes, and make evidence-based environmental decisions.

---

# Unique Selling Proposition (USP)

Current platforms answer:

> "What is the AQI?"

AERIS answers:

- Why did pollution increase?
- Which sources contributed most?
- Which intervention should be implemented?
- What will happen if this policy is applied?
- Which decision provides maximum environmental impact?
- How effective was the implemented action?

# Why AERIS Can Win

Unlike existing platforms that primarily focus on monitoring, forecasting, or awareness, AERIS combines environmental intelligence, explainable AI, policy simulation, and government workflows into a single platform.

This positions AERIS as a decision-support system rather than a monitoring dashboard, addressing one of the largest gaps identified across current air quality platforms.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Existing platforms excel at monitoring.
- Forecasting is becoming increasingly common.
- Decision Intelligence remains largely underserved.
- Governments need actionable insights rather than raw data.
- AI should support environmental decision-making, not just environmental monitoring.
- AERIS differentiates itself by helping authorities decide **what to do next**, rather than simply showing **what is happening**.
